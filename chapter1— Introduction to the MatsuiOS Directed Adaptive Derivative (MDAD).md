# Chapter 1 — Introduction to the MatsuiOS Directed Adaptive Derivative (MDAD)

## 1.1 Overview
The MatsuiOS Directed Adaptive Derivative (MDAD) is a new derivative concept
defined not by fixed directions or fixed scales, but by quantities *generated*
by the MatsuiOS system. These include:

- Direction (generated by OS-dir)
- Scale (generated by OS Geometry)
- Mode (real or complex, depending on singularity avoidance)

While classical derivatives rely on a fixed coordinate system and a fixed
infinitesimal step, MDAD measures local variation along the direction and scale
chosen by the OS based on structural safety, singularity detection, and
adaptive behavior.

## 1.2 Key Features
- The direction of differentiation is generated by the OS.
- The scale of differentiation is generated by the OS.
- The OS automatically switches to complex-step mode near singularities.
- The derivative is defined as the *result* of OS-level decisions.

This enables stable differentiation even near singularities or unstable
regions where classical derivatives fail.
